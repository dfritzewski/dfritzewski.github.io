My research focuses on open clusters as probes for stellar physics, using TESS, Gaia, and soon PLATO which will deliver high-precision, long-term light curves for many open cluster members.

Research

My work addresses fundamental questions about stellar evolution using stars in open clusters as natural laboratories. Below are the main threads of my current research.

🌌

Ensemble Asteroseismology of early-type stars

Asteroseismology probes the stellar interior through the effect of internal waves at the surface. Combining many stars in populations with asteroseismology gives us insights into the distributions of internal stellar properties.

⭐

Rotation and Activity of cool stars

Young cool stars show a wide variety of rotation periods. Connecting rotation and activity on a star-by-star basis in open clusters allows to better understand how magnetic breaking evolves in young stars.

⏳

Stellar ages

Measuring stellar ages accurately is not simple. My research focuses on combining age-determination methods such as gyrochronology, isochrone-fitting, and asteroseismology.
